What is a campaign?

Clicky has two ways to monitor any campaigns or advertisements that you run for your web site. These are called static and dynamic.

Static campaigns

A static campaign is one that is pre-defined in our system. You can enter a landing page or referring domain and any visitor who lands on that page or arrives via that domain will be flagged as a campaign visitor. This method may be preferred if you aren't in complete control of the URL that links to your site, or if your campaign is running on a lot of different sites. By simply entering the URL or referring domain instead, you don't have to change any of your existing links. Click here to setup a static campaign now.

Dynamic campaigns

Dynamic campaigns offer a lot more flexibility, as they do not have to be pre-defined. Instead, you just add a couple of parameters to your landing pages, and we handle the rest. We use the same tags that Google Analytics / Urchin use, so if you are coming from either of those systems, your existing campaigns should work automatically. Please refer to the links below to learn more about setting up dynamic campaigns.

Important notes: There are 5 different parameters you can use to define dynamic campaigns on your landing pages. All of them are optional, except "utm_campaign", which is required. It is essentially the "name" of your campaign, and we group campaigns by their name, so this parameter is always required. Otherwise, all "UTM" parameters will be ignored.

Important notes

  • There are limits on the number of static campaigns you can create, as they take a lot more resources to process than dynamic ones. Your account level allows for up to 30 static campaigns. There are no limits for dynamic campaigns.

  • You don't need to choose between the two campaign types. You can have a mix of both static and dynamic campaigns running at any time, and they will be reported together in the same interface. In other words, while there are two different ways to "define" your campaigns, once they are processed they both end up as the same type of data in your stats.

  • Campaigns are only processed on a visitor's landing page (the first page they view on your site during that session). This helps prevent duplicate hits and assures that the people we log as campaign visitors actually arrived at your site via a campaign.

  • A visitor can only be attached to one campaign. If a visitor's landing page matches more than one static campaign, then only the first one we process will be logged. Be sure to make your matching strings unique enough that they only match the pages they should.

  • Static campaigns override dynamic campaigns. In other words, if a visitor's landing page matches a static campaign, but it also has dynamic campaign variables in the URL, we will only log the static campaign.
